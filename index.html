Multimodal Perceptual Intelligence Lab

Prof. Shweta
Mahajan

Assistant Professor, EECS Department, York University.
Vector Faculty Affiliate, Vector Institute for AI.

I research computer vision and machine learning, with a focus on deep generative models — diffusion models, normalizing flows, variational methods, and GANs — for multimodal representation learning.

Previously a Machine Learning Researcher at Qualcomm AI Research and postdoctoral researcher & Vector Postdoctoral Affiliate at UBC Vision Group, advised by Profs. Leonid Sigal and Kwang Moo Yi. Ph.D. (summa cum laude) from TU Darmstadt under Prof. Stefan Roth.

Research Interests

Diffusion Models

High-level and low-level vision; prompt inversion, view synthesis, personalization, and semantic correspondence.

Multimodal Learning

Cross-modal alignment, vision-language models, story generation, and multimodal representation learning.

Normalizing Flows & VAEs

Exact inference deep generative models, latent space factorization, multi-scale autoregressive priors.

Semantic Correspondence

Unsupervised keypoint detection and semantic correspondence using pretrained diffusion model features.

Autonomous Systems

Distillation of multi-modal LLMs for end-to-end autonomous driving planners.

Efficient Vision Models

Hypernetworks, efficient training strategies, and attention-guided alignment in compact vision-language models.

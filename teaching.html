Teaching

Courses at York University, EECS Department · Lassonde School of Engineering

Teaching Philosophy

I believe in teaching AI and computer vision through the lens of deep mathematical intuition and hands-on implementation. My courses emphasize understanding why methods work, not just how to apply them — building the foundations needed to push the frontier of research.

Current Courses — Winter 2026

EECS 4422 Winter 2026

Computer Vision

Undergraduate / Graduate · 3 Credits · In-person

An introduction to the computational theory and practice of computer vision. Covers image formation, filtering, feature detection and matching, stereo and depth estimation, object recognition, and deep learning for vision tasks including CNNs, vision transformers, and self-supervised methods.

Image Formation Feature Detection Stereo Vision CNNs Object Recognition Vision Transformers

Upcoming — Fall 2026

EECS 6327 Fall 2026

Deep Generative Models

Graduate · 3 Credits · In-person

A graduate course covering the theory and applications of modern deep generative models: variational autoencoders, normalizing flows, generative adversarial networks, and score-based diffusion models. Emphasis on theoretical foundations and applications to multimodal synthesis and representation learning.

VAEs Normalizing Flows GANs Diffusion Models Score Matching Latent Spaces
